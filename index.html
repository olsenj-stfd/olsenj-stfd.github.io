Foundational Ethos

PRINCIPLE I

Community is the infrastructure

The architecture of belonging

The work that matters is rarely the work of one person. For most of a decade, at the San Joaquin Valley Air Pollution Control District, the job was to clean the air for four million people in one of the most polluted, most politically complex basins in the country. Because there are several levers to pull—policy, enforcement, communication, and capital allocation—none of the capital necessary to improve the air moved without understanding the views of community members, business and industry leaders, impact investors, and public health experts.

Beta Testing

A tool built to help climate and impact founders and investors map the regulations, funding, and partners that matter to their venture.

RegScout turns a plain-English description of your venture into a tailored map of the regulatory and funding landscape. 2023

The Participatory Budgeting Tool

Director, Community Strategies & Resources @ Valley Air District

The work I am proudest of. Together with partner agencies, NGOs, industry, and community residents, we built the first participatory budgeting tool for allocating California Climate Investment funds, putting San Joaquin Valley communities in the design seat for clean-air investments in their own neighborhoods. It did in months what years of meetings could not: it got the agency and the community onto the same page, and it was hailed as the program-wide standard.

2021–2025

A $140M+ community portfolio

Director, Community Strategies & Resources @ Valley Air District

We stood up a new impact team from the ground up and stewarded a portfolio of more than $140M across 200+ projects, owning the full arc from strategy to deployment to performance measurement to legislative reporting. The measured result: 8,000 tons of air pollution removed, and a flagship air-purifier program that reached 20,000 homes.

U.S. EPA · Inflation Reduction Act

Authored alongside community partners and won: a successful $1M federal proposal funded through the Inflation Reduction Act, with the dollars pointed squarely at the frontline neighborhoods that needed them.

For kids whose parents work away
